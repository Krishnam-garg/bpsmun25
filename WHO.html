World Health Orginisation
"Child Mortality reduction Strategies."
The World Health Organization (WHO) committee focused on child mortality reduction strategies addresses one of the most pressing global health challenges. With millions of children still dying from preventable causes such as malnutrition, infectious diseases, and lack of access to basic healthcare, this agenda aims to discuss effective, evidence-based strategies to reduce child mortality rates worldwide. Delegates in this session explore the importance of improving healthcare infrastructure, expanding access to immunization, enhancing maternal care, and addressing socio-economic factors that contribute to high mortality rates. The committee emphasizes the need for a collaborative, multi-sectoral approach, where international organizations, governments, and non-governmental entities work together to ensure that every child, regardless of their geographic location, has the opportunity to survive and thrive. Ultimately, the goal is to formulate actionable plans that can save lives, empower communities, and move the world closer to achieving the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal of ending preventable child deaths.
